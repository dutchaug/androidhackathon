GDG Dutch Android User Group

The GDG Dutch Android User Group (DutchAUG) is a a group of Dutch Android professionals who meet and connect with each other on a monthly basis. Our main goal is to inspire and be inspired by hearing members sharing their knowledge about- and experiences with their passion, Android. Our meetups are fun and technical, with presentations done by Android gurus, open discussions, demo's and workshops in an informal setting. The purpose is to provide you "the Dutch Android professional" a place to learn more about the Android technology, ask questions and meet other people. Joining is free!

Droidcon 2013 Hackathon


"You can hack, but you can't hide!"


Location: Science Park, Amsterdam
Date: November 24th, 2013
Event Page: Google+


Are you a savvy Android developer or designer but have little time to play around with all the cool new Google Play APIs and Android features? Or are you lacking inspiration or quality time to think up the next generation Android apps? If you can't hide your enthusiasm for Android and just enjoy building great Android apps this event is for you! Be inspired by your teammates and fellow contestants and measure your Android skills in a single day Android hackathon event, just before the biggest Android conference in The Netherlands, DroidconNL!

Theme: The Android hackathon will be held on Sunday November 24th at the DroidconNL 2013 venue; Science Park Amsterdam. We open the doors at 09:00 and explain the process and rules and start the hackathon a little after that. Then you have until 19:00 to finish up your Android app and present it to a jury of Android experts. The theme will be: "You can hack, but you can't hide!, the smart(watch) way"

News update: Droidcon 2013 Hackathon

Just before DroidconNL 2013 the GDG Dutch Android User Group, is hosting a hackathon for Android developers and designers.

This year's theme will be 'You can hack, but you can't hide! The smart (watch) way'. During this hackathon we will expect about twelve teams, each consisting of two Android developers and one designer, to create some awesome apps and competing for the first place.

From the theme you can read we are hacking away at smart watches! Smart watches have become more and more popular. All major Android hardware manufacturers already have, or are working on their own smart watch. So how cool is it to hack on a smart watch with Android?

Good news, thanks to our sponsors we have been able to procure a limited amount of Sony and I'm Watch smart watches which we can use to make awesome Android apps.
